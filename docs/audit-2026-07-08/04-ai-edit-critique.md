## 1. Retention levers DECIDE already pulls well

These map cleanly onto real TikTok retention mechanics and are stated forcefully enough that Gemini 2.5 Pro will comply:

- **First-1.5s hook discipline.** "the first 1.5 SECONDS decide everything. Open on the most ARRESTING moment… NEVER open on a static silent shot, and NEVER on flat audio (a list of dishes, 'okay so…', throat-clearing)." Concrete positive examples ("this might be the best chicken sandwich I've ever had", "9.5 out of 10") act as mini few-shots — this is the single most important lever and it's handled with real craft, including the audio-mismatch fix: "either TRIM that shot so its audio lands on the strongest nearby line."
- **Open loop with payoff reservation.** The cold-open tease ("promise the payoff up front so they keep watching to find out") paired with the hard rule "NEVER place the shot that delivers the actual final rating/score… in the cold open — that exact shot is RESERVED for the ending; spend it up front and the video has no payoff left." This is a genuine curiosity-gap mechanic, and "using the creator's OWN words (never invent text)" keeps it honest — the same no-fabrication ethic as RetentionRead.
- **Coherence over highlight reel.** "VIRALITY NEVER COMES AT THE COST OF COHERENCE… (a fragmented highlight-reel kills retention)" plus "NEVER interleave two dishes, and NEVER strand a dish's reaction after the next dish has already started." Correct call — mid-video drop-off on food reviews is mostly narrative confusion, not slowness.
- **Peak compression.** "a bite or chew-reaction is best at ~2-4 SECONDS of the PEAK… never ship an 8-second chew." Kills the classic amateur retention leak.
- **Visual-verbal congruence for b-roll.** "B-roll must SHOW what's being said: only place a broll_shot whose depicts_subject (or also_visible) MATCHES the references_subject of the talk_span you're covering. If nothing matches, do NOT place b-roll" — grounded in PERCEIVE's dual index (`shots[]` supply vs `talk_spans[]` demand), which is a genuinely good architecture for this.
- **Payoff-face protection.** "NEVER cover a reaction_kind of bite / first_taste / verdict / peak_reaction with b-roll — the face IS the payoff," and money shots called out as "the most SHAREABLE frames." Correct: shares/rewatches key off faces and drips.
- **Best-take selection + dead-air removal.** "PICK THE BEST, NOT THE FIRST" and `quality_flags` (`dead_air / duplicate_take / false_start / camera_adjust / audio_issue`) as "strong CUT candidates."

## 2. Levers the schema cannot pull (no field exists)

The `EditDecisions` output is: order, cold_open, hook_id, end-trims, voiceovers, b-roll placements, notes. That is the entire actuator space. Missing:

- **Text hook on frame 1 / burned-in overlays.** The single biggest absence. `TextOverlay` is a complete, Codable, WYSIWYG model ("the SAME overlay maps identically onto the 720p preview and the 1080×1920 export") — but DECIDE has no `text_overlays` field. Absurdly, the style pipeline *learns* the habit (`"has_text_overlay": true/false`, `text_and_graphics`) and then StyleConstraintBuilder tells the model to waste it: "Note in edit_note where their usual overlays (e.g. dish names) would go; you are not creating graphics, just flagging placement." A text hook over frame 1 and dish-name/price callouts are among the highest-measured scroll-stop and silent-viewer levers on TikTok, and the render plumbing already exists.
- **Head trims / mid-shot cuts.** `Trim` only has `trim_to_seconds: <absolute proxy second to STOP at>` — an end trim. There is no start trim, so DECIDE cannot cut *into* a line (drop the breath/"okay so" before the punchy word), cannot jump-cut a long talking shot, and its hook fix "TRIM that shot so its audio lands on the strongest nearby line" is only half-executable (it can shorten the tail, never skip a weak head).
- **Pacing curve / speed ramps.** No per-shot `speed` field, even though the client already supports it (EditPlanStore `clipSpeed`, assembler `scaleTimeRange`). DECIDE can only shape pace by shot selection; it cannot compress a slow plating shot 2x or build a fast-cut montage (b-roll is hard-locked to "1.5-3s each" — no 0.5s rapid-fire bursts even when the style profile says `cut_style: fast-punchy`).
- **Loop-back ending.** "ONE GLOBAL VERDICT LAST" is right, but there's no field to end mid-motion or button the last line so the video loops seamlessly into the hook — the cheapest rewatch multiplier. `trimEnd` exists client-side only.
- **Sound design.** No music-bed, SFX, duck, or mute decision (`clipVolume` is client-only), despite the style profile learning `"audio": { "bed": "trending-sound | …", "keeps_natural_food_sounds": … }`. Learned, never actuated.
- **Pattern interrupts / transitions.** No zoom punch-in, freeze, or transition field (CLAUDE.md parks "real crossfades/transitions"; currently hard cuts).
- **B-roll source offset.** `broll_shot_id` names a shot but nothing selects *which slice* of it plays — the sizzle at second 3 of a 10s shot is unreachable.

## 3. Prompt-engineering weaknesses

1. **Dangling references from the pre-DECIDE era — the highest-priority block is partially unexecutable.** BriefPromptBuilder and StyleConstraintBuilder are prepended to *both* the legacy single-call prompt and DECIDE, but their text targets the old `GeminiPrompt.editPlan` schema. In the DECIDE context: "mark the best matching segment keep:true" (DECIDE: "there is no separate keep field"); "put them at the very top of final_edit_order and boost their hook_score" (`hook_score` is a PERCEIVE *input*, not a DECIDE output); "The four strict voiceover_candidate conditions in the body below remain hard requirements" (the body below has no `voiceover_candidate` at all — and the legacy prompt it references lists **three** conditions, not four); "the voiceover rules and segmentation rules in the body below remain hard constraints" and "Tag every segment's section" (segmentation/sections happen in PERCEIVE, which deliberately never sees these blocks). Gemini 2.5 Pro will silently drop these, but they occupy the top of the context, dilute the executable instructions, and can confuse the priority stack.
2. **`propertyOrdering` fights the instructions.** Schema: `["type": "OBJECT", … "propertyOrdering": order, "required": order]` with `order` starting `recommended_duration, recommended_hook, hook_id, cold_open, final_edit_order, trims…`. Gemini structured output emits fields in that order — so the model must commit to `recommended_duration` **before** generating the order and trims, while the prompt says "Add these up as you build final_edit_order… set recommended_duration to your actual result." Pro's internal thinking mitigates this, but the emitted number is structurally pre-selection.
3. **Unverifiable arithmetic with no scratchpad field.** "DURATION BUDGET: each KEPT shot costs (its end − start, or your trim_to_seconds…). Add these up… land WITHIN ±10s" — running-sum accounting over 20-40 shots with no place to show work is exactly what LLMs fumble; there's no `duration_check`/per-shot-cost field and no client-side re-derivation feeding a repair loop (the client *can* compute the true sum from the index — the prompt never tells the model that it will be checked).
4. **No worked few-shot for the hardest behavior.** The tease-vs-verdict distinction ("the tease only HINTS with a stakes/anticipation line… NEVER place the shot that delivers the actual final rating") is the subtlest instruction in the prompt and rests on close reading of `talk_spans`; a single worked mini-index → decisions example would anchor it. Also unresolved edge: multi-dish videos with per-dish ratings — is "8/10 for the fries" mid-video allowed under "The single overall verdict/rating is ALWAYS revealed LAST"? Not stated.
5. **`cold_open` redundancy invites contradiction.** "hook_id <must equal final_edit_order[0]>" and "cold_open [a PREFIX of final_edit_order]" encode the same fact three ways; nothing states `cold_open[0] == hook_id`, and the schema can't enforce the prefix property — a repair path must exist for the disagreement case.
6. **Soft numeric ranges unenforced.** "duration_seconds": <1.5-3> is prose only; the schema is a bare `NUMBER` (no minimum/maximum), and `start_offset_seconds + duration ≤ shot length` ("Keep start_offset + duration INSIDE that shot's length") is unverifiable by the model without arithmetic it isn't asked to show.
7. **The app grades against bands the model was never told.** RetentionRead judges pace `avg < 3.5 → punchy`, length "~25–35s" sweet spot, payoff position `frac >= 0.6 ? .late : .buried` — but DECIDE receives no pacing target when there's no style profile and no sweet-spot guidance beyond the brief's number. A no-style creator can get an edit FirstCutView then labels "Relaxed" / "tightening toward ~30s usually helps" — a self-inflicted misalignment between the grader and the editor.

## 4. Top 5 changes, ranked by retention impact ÷ cost

1. **Add `text_overlays` to the DECIDE schema** (`{text, start/end anchor (shot_id + offset), role: hook|dish_name|price|verdict}`), constrained to the creator's own words per the existing no-fabrication rule. Impact: highest — frame-1 text hook + silent-viewing coverage. Cost: low — `TextOverlay` render path (preview + export burn-in) already ships; only lenient decode + a placement-preset mapping needed.
2. **Add `trim_from_seconds` (head trim) to `Trim`.** Unlocks cutting into the punchy word, breath removal, and true jump-cut pacing; makes the existing hook-trim instruction fully executable. Impact: high (first-3s and mid-video pacing). Cost: low — clips already carry arbitrary source ranges client-side.
3. **Fix the connector blocks + add a self-check field (no client model change).** Rewrite BriefPromptBuilder/StyleConstraintBuilder in DECIDE vocabulary (kill `keep:true`, "boost hook_score", "four voiceover_candidate conditions", "segmentation rules below"); add one worked few-shot; add a `duration_check` string field emitted *before* `final_edit_order` in `propertyOrdering` (or move `recommended_duration` last) so the budget is computed, not vibed. Impact: high on reliability/consistency, which is upstream of every other lever. Cost: near-zero.
4. **Add `ending` directive** (`{style: "loop_to_hook"|"hold_verdict", end_trim_seconds}`) so the last shot cuts on motion/line-button into the opening frame. Impact: medium-high — seamless loops directly inflate completion % and rewatches, TikTok's strongest ranking signals. Cost: low (`trimEnd` exists; loop is just an end-trim choice made intelligently).
5. **Add per-shot `speed`** (e.g. optional `{shot_id, rate: 1.25-2.0}` for non-speech shots). Impact: medium — compresses dead-middle plating/ambiance without cutting content, and enables punchy montage feel. Cost: low — `clipSpeed` + `scaleTimeRange` already threaded through preview and export.

Deferred (worse ratio): music/SFX cues (no client audio-bed pipeline), b-roll source offsets, transitions/pattern-interrupt fields (renderer work parked per CLAUDE.md).
